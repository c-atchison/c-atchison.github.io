Summary

As a professional Software Engineer since 2011, I have never stopped learning.

Starting out in the industrial automation industry, I programmed microprocessors to run Water, Wastewater, and Ozone filtration plants for local municipalities.

I helped create the MACH Alert Fire Station Alerting product that helps dispatchers all over the USA and Canada react to emergencies and mobilize required resources as quickly as possible.

I learned the value of service-oriented architecture, receiving training in XML/XSLT, and maintaining SOAP web services that connected disparate operations for the City of Lakeland.

I developed front end applications to allow business users to run automated ETL processes and create the reports they needed to perform their best.

I designed asynchronous RESTful web services, integrating internal applications with third-party services like USPS, Salesforce, NetSuite, Tableau, Experian, and OneTrust.

I implemented an in-house email alert application to replace WebFocus, saving company funds by removing an expensive subscription.

I performed Data Engineering duties, managing the Snowflake Cloud platform and receiving a SnowPro Core Certification.

Throughout my experience, the ability to quickly injest new technologies and apply them, while also paying heed to the mistakes of the past, has been pivotal to my success. I rarely get to rely on my previous knowledge entirely. There is always something new that drives my curiousity and creative instincts. In the end, software is my vehicle for creation, and my motivation has always been to build applications that I can feel proud of.
